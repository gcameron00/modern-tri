The Philosophy

A challenge for the body.
A reward for the mind.

Endurance sport is brutal on purpose. We kept the brutal part — then we gave it a finish line worth chasing. The result is a discipline that punishes the legs and pampers the spirit.

The Challenge

The run wrings you out. The bike asks for everything your legs have left. By the time the finish line is in sight, every system in your body has been tested — heart rate up, focus sharp, willpower stretched thin. This is real effort, the kind that earns its name. The third leg isn't a shortcut; it's the medal you drink.

The Reward

Then comes the exhale. The mind, flooded with endorphins and the quiet pride of a job finished, finally gets to celebrate. A cocktail at the line isn't indulgence — it's ceremony. It marks the moment the work is done and the joy begins. Body satisfied, mind restored, glass raised.

  • Cardiovascular Glory

    Two legs of genuine aerobic work — your heart logs the miles, no asterisk.

  • Earned Patience

    Delayed gratification, distilled. The wait makes the toast taste better.

  • Mental Clarity

    Effort, then ease. The post-finish glow is the best meditation you'll book.

The Format

Three legs, one shaker

Officially unofficial. Wildly unsanctioned. Here's how a Modern Triathlon runs.

1

The Run — 5 kilometres

Off the start line on foot. Trail, track, or boardwalk; just keep it honest and keep it moving.

2

The Bike — 20 kilometres

Transition to two wheels for a rolling loop. Helmets mandatory, scenery encouraged.

3

The Cocktail — 1 perfectly built drink

Cross the line and assemble your signature serve. Judged on craft, balance, and triumphant presentation.

★

The Golden Rule

Hydrate between every leg. Finish responsibly. The only PB that matters is Personal Best mood.

Ready to chase the finish line?

There's no entry fee, no governing body, and absolutely no swimming. Grab your shoes, pump your tyres, and chill a glass.
